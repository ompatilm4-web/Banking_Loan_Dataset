# Conclusion

1. Employed customers contribute the highest total interest retained, followed by self-employed customers.
2. Interest retained is uniformly distributed across all age groups, with every age contributing exactly $7.8B. There is no age-based differentiation in interest generation.
3. There is a clear positive relationship between loan amount and annual income — customers with higher incomes take larger loans, and vice versa.
4. All employment segments show nearly identical risk scores, ranging from 3.94 to 4.01 risk per customer - a difference of only 1.8%.
5. High-value customers take loans 6.4x larger than low-value customers ($71,115 vs $11,160) - a 537% difference
